üß≠ Why consider Full PPO as the base (even at a higher sticker)

Broader network access reduces out-of-network exposure and ‚Äúprovider hunt‚Äù time, both of which show up as soft costs: missed appointments, delayed care, and morale hits. With Full PPO as the anchor, you cut the number of edge-case exceptions and provider disruptions over the year. That usually lowers ticket volume at HR, trims productivity loss from re-shopping doctors, and improves recruiting when candidates care about specific providers. The member share in Scenario B is actually slightly lower than Scenario A (by about $50/mo), which can help with uptake on preventive care. If you value continuity and provider choice, the richer PPO tends to pay for itself over a renewal cycle through fewer headaches, fewer appeals, and steadier utilization.
